Industry of Interest: Data Analytics

Data Analytics focuses on collecting, organizing, and analyzing data to help organizations make better decisions. It is used to understand customers, improve services, reduce costs, and predict trends.

Career of Interest: Data Analyst / BI Analyst

A Data Analyst (or Business Intelligence Analyst) cleans data, writes queries (SQL), builds dashboards (Power BI), and explains results so leaders can make smart decisions.

Ethical Issue That Could Arise

An ethical issue in this industry is misusing data, such as collecting more personal data than needed, using data without clear permission, or presenting results in a misleading way. Another issue is bias in data, which can lead to unfair decisions.

How My Personal Values Shape My Response

Discipline: I would follow policies, document my work, and avoid shortcuts that reduce accuracy or honesty.

Justice: I would check for bias and speak up if results could treat people unfairly.

Honesty: I would explain limitations clearly and never twist results to match what someone wants.

Kindness/Generosity: I would communicate respectfully and think about how data-based decisions impact real people.

Cybersecurity Issues That Could Arise

Data breaches: Sensitive customer or company data could be stolen.

Phishing: Attackers may trick workers into giving up login credentials to analytics tools.

Ransomware: Systems could be locked, stopping access to databases and dashboards.

Weak access control: Too many people with access increases the chance of leaks or misuse.

Insecure sharing: Downloading data to personal devices or sending files unprotected can expose data.

Cybersecurity Best Practices in Data Analytics

Least privilege: Give users only the access they need.

MFA + strong passwords: Protect accounts for cloud tools and databases.

Encryption: Encrypt sensitive data when stored and when sent.

Secure handling: Use approved storage/sharing tools, not personal email or random USB drives.

Backups: Keep regular backups to recover from ransomware or mistakes.

Training: Teach employees how to spot phishing and suspicious behavior.

Monitoring: Log and monitor access to sensitive datasets to detect unusual activity.
